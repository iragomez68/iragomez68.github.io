Summary: MCI vs. X

This project's purpose is to show if there is a relationship between Major Crime Indicators (MCI) and four factors: household median income, day of week, time of day and premise type. The MCI data was pulled from the Toronto Police Service's Public Data Portal, crossed referenced with the Boundaries Civic Information API to obtain the Ward number (based on the latitude and longitude of the incident happened) and the City of Toronto Open Data Catalog (used for obtaining the household median income for each ward). All 167k crime occurrences from 2014 to 2018 were used for this dataset. Matplotlib was used to make the line plots, which show the relationship between MCI and the four factors.

The data used for this analysis is representative of Major Crime Indicators in the city of Toronto from 2014 to 2018. A longer historical snapshot of data may offer more accurate insight. This sites contain visualizations, analysis, 0.1% of the data source, pivoted dataframes used for calculating the Anova test for each category (household median income, day of the week, time of the day and premise type).
